Grandma.

It was a bright Saturday morning. Splashes of warm sunlight shone through the big, square window next to my bed, onto my quilt, causing a warm toasty scent to fill the room and arouse a feeling of total bliss within me. The gentle babble of flowing water along with the joyous chirping of birds softly stirred me awake. I rubbed my eyes and took a glance at the window: the golden sunlight had already diffused throughout my room, glorious and dazzling. “It was now time to get up for a fresh exciting day.”, my inner voice was telling me. I sat up, swung my legs over the side of the bed, and dressed in the clothes that my grandma had laid out for me the night before. I then jumped down to the floor and ran onto the balcony, where grandma was bending over a large, rectangular concrete sink in the rear corner. She was hand washing our clothes.

“Morning Grandma!”, I shouted, full of energy and excitement.

Grandma turned her head, greeted, and kissed me on both of my cheeks, “Are you hungry, my dear? Let’s have breakfast.”

She stopped what she was doing. We walked together towards the dining room.

This was the scene nearly every day started during the first six years of my early childhood.

Grandma was the soul of my childhood. When I was born, I started living with my grandparents and stayed with them until I was six. The first six years of a child’s life is long enough to shape their personality and build a foundation for that child’s later development. Six years is also short and a fleeting of time within one’s life span. I was the bud, she was the gardener. She continuously fed me with endless love. While I was growing up with Grandma, she was in her early 50’s and retired. She was a wonderful combination of warmth and kindness, laughter, and love, and still is to this day. Her memory will live inside me for all the days of my life.
